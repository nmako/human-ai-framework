- Cite, discuss, and reference the framework in academic publications, journalism, regulatory consultations, NGO advocacy, and policy research

- **Productized tools or platforms built from the framework specification** offered commercially to third parties.
- **Licensing the framework's content into commercial publications** beyond fair-use academic citation.
- **Using the framework's content in commercial software, products, or services** offered to third parties.

If your use falls into any of these categories, contact the maintainer at **natalia@humanaipolicy.org** to discuss commercial licensing terms.

### Edge cases (clarification)

Some uses sit between non-commercial and commercial. The maintainer's interpretation:

- **A company using the framework internally for its own AI governance work** — this is permitted use; companies operating the framework as intended is not a commercial use of the framework itself.
- **A lawyer or consultant who references the framework incidentally in a single client engagement** — this is permitted; incidental reference is not a commercial use of the framework's substance.
- **A consultant whose practice substantively builds on the framework** (presenting it to clients, using it as the basis for assessments, deriving paid services from it) — this requires authorization. This is the core case the commercial license addresses.
- **An NGO providing free advisory services that reference the framework** — this is permitted; non-commercial advisory work is non-commercial use.
- **An academic researcher publishing a study that uses the framework as data** — this is permitted under standard academic citation.

If you are unsure whether your use requires a commercial license, contact **natalia@humanaipolicy.org** before proceeding. Inquiries are responded to within two weeks.
